What is an I-20? (For F-1 Students Only)

Think of your I-20 document as a contract. It indicates your basic biographical information, your major and estimated length of study, School Name, SEVIS ID number, and financial information. DO NOT let your I-20 expire – ISS can extend it for a justifiable reason if more time is needed to complete your program of study.

Your I-20 should always be up-to-date. For example, if your program of study changes or your source of finances changes, a new I-20 with the correct information will be generated. During your time here, you may acquire a number of I-20’s. Do not throw any of them away, rather keep old ones in a safe place.

What is a DS-2019? (For J-1 Students Only)

Think of your DS-2019 document as a contract. It indicates your basic biographical information, your major and length of study, school name, SEVIS ID number, and program sponsor.

DO NOT let your DS-2019 expire – your program sponsor can extend it for a justifiable reason if more time is needed to complete your program of study.

Your DS-2019 should always be up-to-date. For example, if your program dates change, a new DS-2019 with the correct information will be generated.

How do I get my I-20 or DS-2019?

Undergraduate Students

After you are admitted to the University of Kansas, the ISS Document Specialists will review your application and supporting documents. If, after reviewing your application materials, the ISS Document Specialists need any other supporting documents, they will contact you by email to request the needed documents. Afterwards, as long as the ISS Document Specialists have everything they need to create an I-20/DS-2019 from you, they will create your document. The document will be mailed based on the ‘Mailing Options’ listed below.

Graduate Students

After you are admitted to the University of Kansas, you will be emailed your admission letter. On the admission letter, you will be asked to accept your admission offer. Once you accept your admission offer, the ISS Document Specialists will be notified to review your application and supporting documents. If, after reviewing your application materials, the ISS Document Specialists need any other supporting documents, they will contact you by email to request the needed documents. Afterwards, as long as the ISS Document Specialists have everything they need to create an I-20/DS-2019 from you, they will create your document. The document will be mailed based on the ‘Mailing Options’ listed below.

I-20 or DS-2019 Mailing Options
  • Express Mail: You must arrange to use this option within 7 days of email sent to you by the ISS Document Specialist. Express Mail is the quickest option and typically will take about 3-4 days to arrive to you. If you choose to express mail your documents, it will be at your expense. Please go to the following website to set up Express Mail: http://study.eshipglobal.com.
  • Regular Mail: Regular United States Postal Service (USPS) airmail can take up to 3-4 weeks for international mailing. This option is at no cost to you. If you do not set up the Express Mail shipment through eShipGlobal within 7 days of the ISS Document Specialist’s email, your document will automatically be sent by regular USPS mailing.

Please do not request a shipment directly through DHL, UPS, or FedEx. All requests must be completed through eShipGlobal for us to properly receive your mailing selection. You may select the DHL, UPS, or FedEx options within eShipGlobal.

If you use this service, you will be required to create an account. To request a shipment, you will need a mailing address, e-mail, phone number and credit card information. If you have any questions while using this service, please e-mail support@eshipglobal.com.
